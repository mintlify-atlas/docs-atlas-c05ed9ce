## Buffer Settings

Buffer settings control how much video data is preloaded ahead of your current playback position.

### High vs Low Buffer

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="High Buffer (Recommended)">
    **Advantages:**
    - More video preloaded ahead of current position
    - Smooths out network issues and prevents buffering pauses
    - Better experience on unstable connections
    - Minimal impact on RAM usage
    
    **Best for:**
    - Users with unstable internet connections
    - Watching longer videos
    - Preventing playback interruptions
    
    <Tip>
    **Recommendation: High buffer** - The benefits far outweigh any minimal RAM usage increase.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Low Buffer">
    **Advantages:**
    - Slightly lower RAM usage
    - Minimal bandwidth savings if you often close videos early
    
    **Disadvantages:**
    - More likely to pause for buffering
    - Less protection against network hiccups
    - May interrupt playback more frequently
    
    **Best for:**
    - Devices with very limited RAM (rare)
    - Users who frequently skip through videos
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Accordion title="Buffer Setting Location">
  **How to adjust buffer:**
  
  1. Open SmartTube
  2. Go to **Settings**
  3. Navigate to **Video Player** section
  4. Look for **Buffer** options
  5. Select **High** or **Low**
  
  <Info>
  The higher your buffer, the more video will be preloaded. A high buffer increases RAM usage slightly but shouldn't cause issues on modern devices.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Known Buffer Limitations">
  <Warning>
  **Seeking backwards:** When you seek back (e.g., jump back 5 seconds), SmartTube will have to rebuffer. The buffer is not retained when seeking backwards.
  </Warning>
  
  This may be improved in a future update.
</Accordion>

## Background Playback Options

SmartTube supports playing videos while using other apps or when the screen is off.

### Picture-in-Picture (PiP)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Picture-in-Picture">
    Navigate to **Settings** → **General** → **Background playback** → **Picture in picture**
    
    Enable this option to allow videos to continue in a small floating window.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure PiP Activation">
    Set when PiP should activate:
    - **Home button**: Video enters PiP when you press home (default)
    - **Back button**: Video enters PiP when you press back (optional)
    
    Configure in **Settings** → **General** → **Background playback (activation)**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Picture-in-Picture">
    While playing a video:
    - Press the **Home** button (or **Back** if enabled)
    - Video continues in a small window
    - Navigate to other apps while watching
    - Tap the PiP window to return to full screen
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Picture-in-Picture Settings">
  **Available PiP options:**
  
  - **Enable/Disable PiP**: Turn the feature on or off
  - **Activation triggers**: Choose which buttons activate PiP
  - **Window size**: Some devices allow PiP window size adjustment
  - **Position**: PiP window position on screen (device dependent)
  
  <Info>
  PiP support and options vary by device and Android version. Some features may not be available on older devices.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

### Background Audio

<Accordion title="Audio Playback When Screen Is Off">
  Background audio allows music or podcasts to continue playing with the screen off.
  
  **Configuration:**
  1. Go to **Settings** → **General** → **Background playback**
  2. Enable background audio options
  3. Choose screen-off behavior
  
  **Use cases:**
  - Music videos and playlists
  - Podcasts and interviews
  - Saving battery on long audio content
</Accordion>

## Customization Options

### Theme and UI Settings

<Accordion title="Theme Options">
  SmartTube offers multiple theme choices for different preferences and viewing conditions.
  
  **Available themes:**
  - **Dark theme** (default) - Best for low-light environments
  - **Light theme** - Better for bright environments
  - **Black/OLED theme** - Pure black backgrounds for OLED displays
  - **Custom color themes** - Various color scheme options
  
  **How to change theme:**
  1. Go to **Settings** → **Interface**
  2. Select **Theme** or **Appearance**
  3. Choose your preferred theme
  4. Theme applies immediately
  
  <Tip>
  OLED users: Use the pure black theme to save power and reduce screen burn-in risk.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Interface Scale and Layout">
  Adjust UI size and layout to match your screen size and viewing distance.
  
  **Options:**
  - **UI scale**: Make interface elements larger or smaller
  - **Card size**: Adjust thumbnail/card dimensions
  - **Text size**: Increase or decrease text readability
  - **Sidebar position**: Customize menu placement
  
  **Configuration:**
  - Navigate to **Settings** → **Interface**
  - Adjust scale and layout options
  - Changes apply immediately
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Custom Button Configuration">

## Playback Settings

### Autoplay Modes

<Accordion title="Configuring Autoplay Behavior">
  Control what happens when a video ends.
  
  **How to change autoplay mode:**
  - While playing a video, press the **loop button** 🔁
  - Cycles through different autoplay modes
  
  **Available modes:**
  - **Autoplay on**: Next video starts automatically
  - **Autoplay off**: Video stops after each video ends
  - **Repeat one**: Current video loops continuously
  - **Repeat all**: Playlist or queue loops continuously
  
  <Tip>
  You can quickly switch between autoplay modes using the loop button in the player interface.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

### Playback Speed

<Accordion title="Adjusting Playback Speed">
  Change video playback speed for faster or slower viewing.
  
  **How to adjust:**
  1. While playing a video, press the **speed indicator** icon (gauge) in the top row
  2. Select desired speed (0.25x to 2x or higher)
  3. Speed is remembered across videos
  
  **Common speeds:**
  - **0.5x**: Slow motion, tutorials
  - **1.0x**: Normal speed
  - **1.25x**: Slightly faster, comfortable for most content
  - **1.5x**: Faster pacing, good for podcasts
  - **2.0x**: Very fast, for familiar content
  
  <Warning>
  **Known issue:** Some playback speeds may cause frame drops. This is a known limitation and cannot currently be fixed.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

## Update Settings

SmartTube includes a built-in updater for easy updates.

<Accordion title="Update Configuration">
  **Built-in updater features:**
  
  - Automatic update checks (few seconds after launch)
  - Changelog display
  - Manual update option
  - Beta vs Stable channel selection
  
  **How to configure:**
  1. Go to **Settings** → **About**
  2. Choose update preferences:
     - Enable/disable automatic update checks
     - Select update channel (beta or stable)
  3. **Manual update**: Tap "Check for updates" to update immediately
  
  <Info>
  You only need to manually install SmartTube once. All future updates can be done through the built-in updater.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Update Troubleshooting">
  **If update fails:**
  
  1. **Check disk space**: Most common cause - free up storage
  2. **Retry download**: Update may not have downloaded correctly
  3. **Manual update**: Go to **Settings** → **About** → **Check for updates**
  4. **Verify storage**: Install to internal memory, not SD card
  
  <Warning>
  Updates require sufficient disk space. Clear up storage if installation fails.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

### Auto Frame Rate (AFR)

<Accordion title="What is AFR?">
  **AFR = Auto Frame Rate**
  
  It adjusts your TV's refresh rate to match the content you're watching.
  
  **Benefits:**
  - Slightly smoother playback
  - Eliminates judder on some displays
  - The difference is very small
  
  **Drawbacks:**
  - Doesn't work well on all hardware
  - May cause screen blanking during transitions
  - Most people barely notice the improvement
  
  <Tip>
  **Recommendation:** You can turn AFR on to see if it works on your device. If it causes issues or you don't care to test, turn it **off**.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>
