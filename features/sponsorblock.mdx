SmartTube includes built-in SponsorBlock integration, allowing you to automatically skip sponsored segments and other unwanted parts of videos.

## What is SponsorBlock?

<Info>
SponsorBlock is an open-source crowdsourced project that lets you skip sponsor segments in YouTube videos using a privacy-preserving query system.
</Info>

From the [SponsorBlock website](https://sponsor.ajay.app/):

> SponsorBlock is an open-source crowdsourced browser extension and open API for **skipping sponsor segments** in YouTube videos. The extension automatically skips sponsors **it knows about** using a privacy preserving query system. It also supports skipping **other categories**, such as intros, outros and reminders to subscribe [and non-music parts in music videos].

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Community Submissions">
    Users around the world watch videos and submit timestamps for different segment types (sponsors, intros, etc.) to the SponsorBlock database.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Privacy-Preserving Queries">
    When you watch a video, SmartTube queries the SponsorBlock API using only the video ID - no personal information is shared.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic Skipping">
    If segments are found in the database, SmartTube automatically skips them based on your configured preferences.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Seamless Playback">
    The video continues playing smoothly, as if the unwanted segments never existed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Segment Categories

SponsorBlock can skip multiple types of segments. You can choose which categories to skip in SmartTube's settings:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sponsor" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Paid promotion segments**
    
    When a creator promotes a product or service in exchange for payment or sponsorship. This is the most common category.
    
    Example: "This video is sponsored by NordVPN..."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Unpaid/Self Promotion" icon="bullhorn">
    **Unpaid promotions**
    
    When a creator promotes their own products, services, or other channels without direct payment from a third party.
    
    Example: "Check out my merch store..."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Interaction Reminder" icon="bell">
    **Reminders to like, subscribe, or follow**
    
    Segments where creators ask viewers to like, subscribe, follow on social media, or engage with the video.
    
    Example: "Don't forget to hit that like button and subscribe!"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Intermission/Intro Animation" icon="film">
    **Intro sequences**
    
    Intro animations, channel branding, or segments that occur at the start of videos before the main content.
    
    Example: Animated logo sequences or standard intro clips
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Endcards/Credits" icon="credit-card">
    **Outro sequences**
    
    Credits, end cards, or outros at the end of videos after the main content has finished.
    
    Example: "Thanks for watching" segments with suggested videos
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Preview/Recap" icon="rotate-left">
    **Previews and recaps**
    
    Quick previews of what's coming in the video, or recaps of previous episodes/videos.
    
    Example: "In today's video we'll be..." or "Last time on..."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Music: Non-Music Section" icon="music">
    **Non-music sections in music videos**
    
    Parts of music videos that don't contain music, such as talking segments or skits.
    
    Example: Dialogue or story segments in music videos
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Filler Tangent" icon="comment-dots">
    **Tangential content**
    
    Tangential scenes or jokes that aren't required to understand the main content.
    
    Example: Long unrelated stories or extended jokes
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Configure SponsorBlock

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open SmartTube Settings">
    Launch SmartTube and navigate to the Settings menu (gear icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find SponsorBlock Settings">
    Look for the SponsorBlock section in the settings menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Categories">
    Choose which segment categories you want to skip automatically. You can enable or disable each category individually.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Skip Behavior">
    Choose whether segments should be:
    - **Auto-skip**: Automatically skipped without notification
    - **Show in seek bar**: Highlighted but not skipped
    - **Manual skip**: Show a skip button you can choose to press
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Enjoy">
    Your preferences are saved automatically. Start watching videos and SponsorBlock will work in the background!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Start by enabling just the "Sponsor" category to see how it works, then add other categories based on your preferences.
</Tip>

## Limitations

<Warning>
SmartTube's SponsorBlock integration has some limitations compared to the browser extension.
</Warning>

### Cannot Submit Segments

**You cannot submit new segments** from SmartTube. TVs and TV remotes aren't great devices for precise timestamp operations required for segment submission.

**Why?**
- Requires precise timestamp marking
- Needs detailed category selection
- TV interfaces aren't optimized for this workflow
- Remote controls lack precision for frame-accurate editing

**Alternative**: If you want to contribute segments, use:
- The SponsorBlock browser extension on desktop
- The YouTube ReVanced app on mobile

### Depends on Community

SponsorBlock is a **free and voluntary project** based on user submissions.

<Note>
Don't expect SponsorBlock to work 100% of the time. The effectiveness depends on:
- Whether someone has watched and submitted segments for that video
- How recently the video was uploaded
- The video's popularity and view count
</Note>

**Common scenarios:**
- **New videos**: May not have segments yet (usually added within hours/days for popular channels)
- **Unpopular videos**: May never receive segment submissions
- **Server issues**: SponsorBlock servers can occasionally be offline or overloaded
- **Inaccurate segments**: Sometimes timestamps may be slightly off (community voting helps improve accuracy)

## How SponsorBlock Protects Privacy

SponsorBlock is designed with privacy in mind:

1. **No account required** - works anonymously
2. **Only video IDs are sent** - no personal information
3. **No tracking** - doesn't monitor your viewing habits
4. **Open source** - code is publicly auditable
5. **Minimal data** - only queries what's necessary

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why didn't SponsorBlock skip a sponsor?">
    Possible reasons:
    - The segment hasn't been submitted yet (especially for new videos)
    - SponsorBlock servers are temporarily unavailable
    - The category is disabled in your settings
    - The video is too new or unpopular for submissions
    
    Check your internet connection and settings. For new videos, try again later.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I see what was skipped?">
    Yes! Look at the video seek bar - skipped segments are typically highlighted in different colors based on their category. You can also manually seek to those segments if you want to watch them.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Will this work on live streams?">
    SponsorBlock works best on VODs (video on demand). For live streams:
    - **During live**: No segments available yet
    - **After stream ends**: Segments may be added by the community
    - **Stream archives**: Full SponsorBlock support
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I report incorrect segments?">
    Not directly from SmartTube due to TV interface limitations. However:
    - Incorrect segments are often downvoted by the community
    - Community moderation helps maintain quality
    - Most segments are accurate due to voting systems
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Does this violate creator rights?">
    SponsorBlock is legal and doesn't violate copyright:
    - You still watch the full video file
    - Creators retain all their content
    - It's similar to manually skipping forward
    - Many creators support viewer choice
    
    However, sponsorships help creators financially, so consider supporting them through other means.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How accurate are the segments?">
    Generally very accurate! The community voting system ensures:
    - Bad submissions get downvoted
    - Good submissions get upvoted
    - Multiple submissions are averaged
    - Trusted users' submissions are weighted higher
    
    Occasionally you may encounter slightly off timestamps, but these are rare.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
